The Architecture of Silence.

In the digital age, we have forgotten the weight of the pause. We navigate through a constant stream of algorithmic noise, where silence is treated as a void to be filled rather than a space to be inhabited. This essay explores how contemporary literature is reclaiming the "white space" of the page as a radical act of resistance against the attention economy.

Salt & Cedar

By Julian Vane

The tide retreats
carrying the weight
of heavy stones
and broken light.
We are left
on the damp sand
holding the echo
of what was salt.
